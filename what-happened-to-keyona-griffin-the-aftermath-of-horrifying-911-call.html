What Happened To Keyona Griffin?

Keyona Griffin was one of the victims of the Michigan Family tragedy who called 911 to report the incident before being killed herself.

65-year-old Jacqueline Baber-Bey, 25, and Keyona Griffin, 47, live at 553 Sheldon in Grand Rapids, Michigan. Cherletta Baber-Bey and her lover, 47-year-old “Jay,” shared a residence.

A horrifying tragedy struck the family last month, resulting in a massacre. Here’s everything we know so far.

Who is Keyona Griffin?

Keyona Griffin is the woman who called 911 to report her aunt’s death and her possible murder. Three Grand Rapids Police officers arrived at 553 Sheldon seven minutes and 41 seconds after the call between Keyona and 911 ended.

The Grand Rapids Police Department released bodycam video of the officers’ arrival. They failed after numerous attempts to enter the house and did not attempt to contact anyone in the interim.

The police in Grand Rapids never made contact with anyone inside the house. They departed three minutes and 42 seconds after arriving. Two hours and 18 minutes later, 911 dispatchers received a second call from the same residence.

According to Target 8, Cherletta’s family described her as a loving, trustworthy, and lovely woman who avoided social situations. “Jay” was her first true love, and they spent the majority of their time together in her bedroom.

During the murder investigation, detectives discovered a box of letters in Cherletta’s closet. Between her and Derrell Brown, it was a series of “prison letters.” In those letters, Derrell stated that he went by the name “Jay.” It was the signature on every letter.
