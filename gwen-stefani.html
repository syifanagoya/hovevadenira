Who is Gwen Stefani? 

Every day we see the new artist, new face, new songs and movies. It's all about the hype of being famous, living a celebrity life. Gwen Stefani is a great American public figure who is known for her songs, portrayals and movies too. She has sold over 30 million albums till date.

Gwen Stefani is an American singer, songwriter, actress, television personality, and fashion designer. She is also popular as the co-founder of the rock band No Doubts. Moreover, Stefani is also known as the Grammy Award winner singer.

Gwen Renee Stefani was born on 3rd Octobver 1969, Fullerton, California, the United States of America, where another American singer Carnie Wilson was born. 

Born to her American parents Dennis Stefani and Patti Fynn, she is of White ethncity and holds American nationality. Gwen's father serves as a markieting executive for Yamaha, and mother is a former accountant. 

She grew up with her two small siblings named Jill Stefani and Todd Stefani, and an elder brother named Eric Stefani. Gwen's interest in music began since her early age, when her parents introduced her to folk music and artists like Emmylou Harris and Bob Dylan.

Talking about her education, Stefani attended Loara High School located in Anaheim;. Later in 1987, Gwen joined Fullerton College before shifting to California State University.

After graduation, Stefani began her singing carreer as a singer for her brother Eric's band No Doubt. In 1992, No Doubt, released their first self-titled album, however, the album was not successful. 

After several years in 2000, Gwen's band released another album named Return of Saturn which flawed their ska punk & reggae influences. The lyrics also represented the singer's relationship with Bush frontman Gavin Rossdale who was once in a relationship with Pearl Lowe.

Additionally, Gwen runs her own fashion line named L.A.M.B, which produces fashion accessories, apparel, and perfumes named 'L'. 

Moreover, in 2016, Stefani announced about the production of an animated series about her and the Harajuku Girls, with Moody Street Kids and Vision Animation. Additionally, Gwen assisted to create the show featuring herself such as Music, Love Angel and HJ5.

Gwen Stefani Second Relationship

Several years after her bereakup with Tony, Gwen met guitarist and lead singer of the band Bush Gavin Rossdale in 1995. The pair married on 14th September 2002. The wedding took place at St. Paul's, Covent Garden, London. The couple also had their second wedding  in Los Angeles after two weeks. 

Since the marriage, the couple shares three children together, Kingston Rossdale (born in 2006), Apollo Bowie Flynn (born in 2014), and Zuma Nesta Rock Rossdale (born in 2008). 

On 3rd August 2015, Gwen filed for divorce against Gavin Rossdale. She mentioned ''irreconciable differences'' as the reason for divorce. Om 8th April 2016, the divorce was finalized, as Gavin agreed to ''unequal split'' of their wealth.

Gwen makes a huge money fom her career as a singer, actress, and television personality. Till date she has numerous songs, played in several movies and television shows which adds up to her wealth. 

She has sold more than 30 million albums worldwide and was listed among 100 greatest women in music. Stefani being a judge of The Voice, she reportedly earns $13 million per season. Gwen Stefani's total net worth is estimated to be $120 million, which is more than another American singer Alecia Demner with a wealth of $1 million.

Charity

Besides her music and business career, Gwen is actively involved in charity. After the 2011 Tohoku earthquake and tsunami, Gwen donated $1 million to Save the Children's Japan Earthquake-Tsunami Children in Emergency Fund. She ran an auction on eBay from 11th April to 25th April, 2011, which allowed participants to bid on vintage clothing items from her personal wardrobe and custom T-shirts signed and designed by her. 

Moreover, at the amfAR gala at 2011 Cannes Film Festival, Gwen auctioned of the lacy black dress she wore at the event for charity and raised more than $125,000.
