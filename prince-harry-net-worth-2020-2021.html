Early Life

Henry Charles Albert David or Prince Henry of Wales, commonly known as “Prince Harry” was born on September 15, 1984, in St. Mary’s Hospital, London. He is the younger son of Charles, Prince of Wales, and Diana, Princess of Wales. Prince Harry grew up with his elder brother, Prince William.

Princess Diana made sure that they are bought up like normal kids and not like previous royal kids, so she took them to places like McDonalds and Disneyworld. Prince Harry did nursery at Jane Mynors’ nursery school and the pre-preparatory Wetherby School in London.

Prince Harry then attended Ludgrove School and after clearing the entrance, was admitted to Eton College. He graduated from Eton College with two A-levels. He was brilliant in sports, especially polo and rugby union. There was a rumor that Harry was a weak student, and the college management helped him to get the grades.

After receiving A-class, he was eligible to apply for an officer commission in the British Army. After graduating, Harry took a year gap and spent some time in Australia for the Polo test match. He also traveled to Lesotho, where he made the documentary ‘The Forgotten Kingdom.’

Prince of Wales had a very interesting dating life. For six years, he was in an on-and-off relationship with Chelsy Davy, but the long-distance relationship couldn’t work out, and they broke up. After that, he was romantically involved with a British actress Cressida Bonas, but within two years, they terminated their relationship.

Currently, Prince Harry is in a relationship with Meghan Markle, a Hollywood actress. The couple is going out for almost two years now and has decided to get married soon.

Career

After being eligible, Prince Harry got himself enrolled at the Royal Military Academy Sandhurst to undergo officers training. After completing such a tough training, he was commissioned in the Blues and Royals as a Cornet that is the second lieutenant. When his training was finished, he was rumored to be deployed in Iraq.

It was a very debatable topic at that time, whether or not a prince should engage in war. General Sir Richard Dannatt decided that Prince Harry will be going to war. But, a month later, General Sir Richard Dannatt decided not to put him in the war as he will be the obvious target and will affect the other soldiers around him.

Prince Harry was deployed as a forward air controller in Afghanistan’s Helmand Province. He was the first British Royal member to be deployed in the war zone, and Diana couldn’t have been more proud of it. After returning, he became lieutenant and received an Operational Service Medal for Afghanistan for his military service.

He then began his training in the Defense Helicopter flying High School, after earning his Apache Flying badge, soon he was promoted to a Captain. Prince Harry was sent with Army Air Corps on a tour duty as a co-pilot and gunner for Apache helicopter. After that, the Ministry of Defense decided that he is qualified as an Apache Helicopter Commander.

Prince Harry is also the Knight Commander of the Royal Victorian Order (KCVO). He ended his official duties with armed forces two years back.

Being born in a Royal family, one of his honor is his birthright, i.e., “His Royal Highness Prince Henry of Wales.” One his biggest achievements, other than the military titles, would be being Knighted by his grandmother, the Queen.
