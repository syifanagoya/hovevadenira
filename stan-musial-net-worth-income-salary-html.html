Stanislaw Franciszek Musia (born November 21, 1920, in St. Louis, Missouri, USA) was an American professional baseball player who played for the St. Louis Cardinals for 22 seasons from 1941 to 196Musial was a Navy veteran of World War II who played outfield and first base in the Major League Baseball (MLB) for the Cardinals from 1941 to 196Stan Musial, well known by his nickname “Stan the Man,” is generally regarded as one of the best hitters in baseball history. He finished his career with 3,630 hits, good for fourth all-time and first in a career in which he played for just one club. He is also regarded to be the most consistent hitter of his generation, having collected 1,815 hits at home and 1,815 on the road. During his career, he hit 475 home runs and was awarded the National League’s (NL) Most Valuable Player (MVP) three times. He also won three World Series championships with the New York Yankees. He, along with Hank Aaron and Willie Mays, holds the Major League Baseball record for the most All-Star Games he has participated in (24). On the first ballot of the Baseball Hall of Fame induction, Musial received his honour in 196Musial was born in Donora, Pennsylvania, where he grew up playing baseball both informally and in organised settings, ultimately making it onto the baseball team at Donora High School. 

He was also inducted into the St. Louis Cardinals Hall of Fame in the inaugural class of 201Musial was originally signed to a professional contract by the St. Louis Cardinals as a pitcher in 1938, but he was converted to outfielder before to making his big league debut with the team in 194He immediately established himself as a reliable and prolific hitter, which was attributed to his distinctive hitting posture. The Cardinals won the World Series in 1942, during his first full season as manager. The next season, he topped the National League in six major offensive categories and was named the league’s Most Valuable Player. In addition, he was selected to the National League All-Star team for the first time; he would go on to appear in every All-Star game (MLB had two games from 1959 to 1962) in every season after that in which he participated. Having won his second World Series title in 1944, Musial was called up to serve in the United States Navy for the full 1945 season. When he returned to baseball in 1946, Musial was able to regain his regular hitting form. That year, he won his second MVP award as well as his third World Series championship. His third MVP title came in 1948, when he fell one home run short of being the first player to capture the Triple Crown of baseball. Musial hired a personal trainer to help him stay productive after struggling offensively in 1959.
