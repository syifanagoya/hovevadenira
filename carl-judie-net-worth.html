Introduction

Carl Judie was an American actor whose career started much later in life due to his 20-year military career.

He was mostly known as a theater actor, but he was best known as a television actor.

He died on February 14, 2021.

The Origin Story

He was born in the west Texas city of Lubbock on July 11, 1958, and his family remained there throughout his childhood.

His parents were Roscoe Judie and Dessia Lee of Lubbock, Texas.

There is not a lot known about his childhood. However, he did attend school in the Lubbock Independent School district where the family lived.

He attended and graduated from Estacado High School, but not much is known other than that.

The College Years

He attended and graduated from Texas Tech University located in Lubbock, Texas, and shortly after he finished school, he joined the military.

Judie served 20+ years before retiring from military service, and he decided to take up acting.

Career Accomplishments

After spending 20 years serving his country in the military, Carl got his acting break in the theater.

In 2013, he accepted a fill-in role as the lead in his wife’s stage play.

This led to many other roles, and he would eventually continue to perform in the theater.

After a six-year stint on the stage, he sought other opportunities for his acting career.

He had several television roles over his career, and he also joined Dhar Mann Studios, which specialized in short movies.

Many movie shorts found their way on YouTube and became pretty lucrative.

He was best known for his role as Grandpa in the movie short drama called A Menstrual Show. It was written, directed, and starred Joshua Daniel.

Wife

Carl Judie dated and eventually married his high school sweetheart Sharon sometime after graduation in 1981.

They only were married for a short time and eventually divorced also in 1981.

Despite being married for a short time they did have a daughter of their own.

Death

Judie passed away on February 16, 2021, at the age of 63.
