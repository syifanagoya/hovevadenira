Jonathan Goldstein Full Overview and Wiki

Early Beginnings to Radio Stardom

Jonathan Goldstein’s journey from the telemarketing trenches to becoming one of the top-tier humorists and storytellers of his generation is nothing short of inspiring.

Born to Buzz and Dina Goldstein in Brooklyn, New York, and raised in Montreal, Goldstein’s early life was steeped in the rich cultural tapestry of his family’s Canadian-American roots.

His academic path through McGill University and later, a Master’s program in Creative Writing at Concordia University, laid the groundwork for what would become a prolific career.

A Foray into Public Radio

Goldstein’s foray into radio, beginning with his work on This American Life, saw him relocate to Chicago and immerse himself in storytelling and production.

His contributions here, and later as the host of CBC’s WireTap, showcased his unique voice and ability to connect with listeners.

His move back to Montreal to start WireTap marked the beginning of a significant chapter in his career, blending humor, introspection, and life’s oddities into compelling radio.

FAQs about Jonathan Goldstein

Who is Jonathan Goldstein?

Jonathan Goldstein is an American-Canadian author, humorist, and radio producer known for his work on radio programs and podcasts such as Heavyweight, This American Life, and WireTap.

What notable works has he produced?

Goldstein’s notable works include the podcast Heavyweight, contributions to This American Life, and hosting the CBC Radio One program WireTap from 2004 to 2015.

Where was he born?

He was born in Brooklyn, New York City, New York, on August 22, 1969.

What is his educational background?

Goldstein attended McGill University and later completed a master’s program in creative writing at Concordia University.

How did Jonathan Goldstein start his career?

After graduation, Goldstein worked in the telemarketing industry for ten years while continuing to write and attend readings. His career received a significant boost after being selected to work on Ira Glass’ popular public radio program This American Life.

What is Heavyweight about?

Heavyweight, a podcast started by Goldstein in September 2016 with Gimlet Media, features stories of people confronting unresolved issues or moments from their past.

Has he received any academic attention for his work?

Yes, Goldstein’s work has been academically examined as representative of the positioning of Jews and Canadians as potentially overlooked minorities in the late-twentieth- and early twenty-first-century United States.

What other projects has Jonathan Goldstein been involved in?

In addition to his radio and podcast work, Goldstein played an expert witness in humorist John Hodgman’s comedy/court show podcast Judge John Hodgman in May 2014.

What roles has he held in the radio industry?

Goldstein has contributed to various radio shows, hosted WireTap, and served as a producer on This American Life. He is also a member of the Public Radio Exchange editorial board.

Where did he grow up?

Although born in Brooklyn, Goldstein spent the first four years of his life there before moving with his family to Montreal, Quebec, his mother’s hometown, where they settled in the suburb of Laval.
